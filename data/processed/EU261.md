# Regulation (EC) No 261/2004 — Passenger Rights in Air Travel

Common rules on compensation and assistance to passengers in the event of denied boarding, cancellation, or long delay of flights.

---

# 1 SCOPE

## 1.1 FLIGHTS COVERED

This Regulation applies to passengers departing from an airport in an EU Member State, and to passengers departing from an airport outside the EU to an airport in an EU Member State, provided the operating carrier holds a valid operating licence granted by a Member State (a Community carrier) and the passenger did not already receive compensation or assistance in the country of departure.

The Regulation applies only to motorised fixed-wing aircraft.

## 1.2 PASSENGERS COVERED

To be entitled to rights under this Regulation, a passenger must:
- hold a confirmed reservation on the flight concerned, and
- have presented themselves for check-in at the time indicated in writing by the carrier, tour operator, or travel agent — or, if no time was indicated, no later than 45 minutes before the published departure time.

Passengers who have been transferred by a carrier or tour operator from their original flight to another flight are also covered, regardless of the reason for the transfer.

This Regulation does not apply to passengers travelling free of charge or on a reduced fare not available to the general public. It does apply to passengers travelling on tickets issued under a frequent flyer programme or other commercial programme.

---

# 2 DENIED BOARDING

Denied boarding means a refusal by the carrier to carry a passenger who has presented themselves for boarding in accordance with section 1.2, except where there are reasonable grounds such as health, safety, security, or inadequate travel documentation.

When a flight is overbooked, the carrier must first call for volunteers willing to give up their seat in exchange for agreed benefits. Only if an insufficient number of volunteers comes forward may the carrier deny boarding to passengers against their will.

## 2.1 ASSISTANCE IN THE EVENT OF DENIED BOARDING

If you are denied boarding against your will, you are entitled to choose between:
- re-routing to your final destination under comparable transport conditions at the earliest opportunity, or at a later date at your convenience subject to seat availability; or
- full reimbursement within seven days of the ticket price for the part of the journey not made, and for the part already made if the flight no longer serves any purpose in relation to your original travel plan, together with a return flight to your first point of departure where relevant.

You are also entitled free of charge to:
- meals and refreshments in reasonable relation to the waiting time;
- hotel accommodation if an overnight or additional stay becomes necessary, including transport between the airport and the hotel;
- two telephone calls, telex or fax messages, or e-mails.

If the carrier re-routes you to an alternative airport serving the same destination, the carrier must cover the cost of transferring you to the original airport or to another nearby destination agreed with you.

## 2.2 COMPENSATION IN THE EVENT OF DENIED BOARDING

If you are denied boarding against your will, you are entitled to financial compensation paid immediately at the airport:

- Flights of 1,500 km or less: **EUR 250**
- Intra-EU flights over 1,500 km, and all other flights between 1,500 and 3,500 km: **EUR 400**
- All other flights: **EUR 600**

The compensation may be reduced by 50% if you are offered re-routing and your actual arrival at the final destination does not exceed the scheduled arrival time of the original flight by more than 2 hours (flights ≤1,500 km), 3 hours (flights 1,500–3,500 km), or 4 hours (all other flights).

Distances are measured by the great circle route method, based on the last destination where denial of boarding delays the passenger's arrival.

Compensation is paid in cash, by electronic bank transfer, bank order, or bank cheque. With your signed agreement, it may be paid in travel vouchers or other services.

# 3 CANCELLATION

Cancellation means the non-operation of a flight that was previously planned and on which at least one seat was reserved.

## 3.1 ASSISTANCE IN THE EVENT OF CANCELLATION

If your flight is cancelled, you are entitled to choose between:
- re-routing to your final destination under comparable transport conditions at the earliest opportunity, or at a later date at your convenience subject to seat availability; or
- full reimbursement within seven days of the ticket price for the part of the journey not made, and for the part already made if the flight no longer serves any purpose in relation to your original travel plan, together with a return flight to your first point of departure where relevant.

You are also entitled free of charge to:
- meals and refreshments in reasonable relation to the waiting time;
- hotel accommodation if an overnight or additional stay becomes necessary (including transport between the airport and the hotel) — this applies when the new flight departs the day after the original scheduled departure;
- two telephone calls, telex or fax messages, or e-mails.

The carrier must provide an explanation of possible alternative transport when informing you of the cancellation.

If the carrier re-routes you to an alternative airport serving the same destination, the carrier must cover the cost of transferring you to the original airport or to another nearby destination agreed with you.

## 3.2 COMPENSATION IN THE EVENT OF CANCELLATION

If your flight is cancelled, you are entitled to financial compensation unless:
- you are informed at least 14 days before scheduled departure; or
- you are informed between 7 and 14 days before departure and offered re-routing departing no more than 2 hours before and arriving no more than 4 hours after the originally scheduled times; or
- you are informed less than 7 days before departure and offered re-routing departing no more than 1 hour before and arriving no more than 2 hours after the originally scheduled times; or
- the cancellation was caused by extraordinary circumstances that could not have been avoided even if all reasonable measures had been taken (e.g. severe weather, political instability, security risks, unexpected safety shortcomings, air traffic management decisions, or strikes affecting the carrier). The carrier bears the burden of proving both the extraordinary nature of the circumstances and that all reasonable measures were taken.

Where compensation is due, the amounts are:

- Flights of 1,500 km or less: **EUR 250**
- Intra-EU flights over 1,500 km, and all other flights between 1,500 and 3,500 km: **EUR 400**
- All other flights: **EUR 600**

The compensation may be reduced by 50% if you are offered re-routing and your actual arrival at the final destination does not exceed the scheduled arrival time of the original flight by more than 2 hours (flights ≤1,500 km), 3 hours (flights 1,500–3,500 km), or 4 hours (all other flights).

Distances are measured by the great circle route method, based on the last destination where the cancellation delays the passenger's arrival.

Compensation is paid in cash, by electronic bank transfer, bank order, or bank cheque. With your signed agreement, it may be paid in travel vouchers or other services.

This Regulation does not apply where a package tour is cancelled for reasons other than the cancellation of the flight itself.

# 4 DELAY

## 4.1 ASSISTANCE IN THE EVENT OF DELAY

The following assistance applies when the carrier reasonably expects departure to be delayed beyond the scheduled time by:
- 2 hours or more for flights of 1,500 km or less;
- 3 hours or more for intra-EU flights over 1,500 km and all other flights between 1,500 and 3,500 km;
- 4 hours or more for all other flights.

You are entitled free of charge to:
- meals and refreshments in reasonable relation to the waiting time;
- hotel accommodation if an overnight or additional stay becomes necessary (including transport between the airport and the hotel) — this applies when the new expected departure time is the day after the previously announced departure;
- two telephone calls, telex or fax messages, or e-mails.

If the delay is at least 5 hours, you may additionally choose to abandon travel and receive full reimbursement within seven days of the ticket price for the part of the journey not made, and for the part already made if the flight no longer serves any purpose in relation to your original travel plan, together with a return flight to your first point of departure where relevant.

Note: The right to financial compensation for delays of 3 hours or more at arrival is established by Court of Justice of the EU case law (Sturgeon, C-402/07), not by the text of this Regulation itself.

---

# 5 DOWNGRADING

If the carrier places you in a lower class than the one for which you purchased your ticket, you are entitled to reimbursement within seven days of:
- 30% of the ticket price for flights of 1,500 km or less;
- 50% of the ticket price for intra-EU flights over 1,500 km (except flights between EU Member States and French overseas departments) and all other flights between 1,500 and 3,500 km;
- 75% of the ticket price for all other flights, including flights between EU Member States and French overseas departments.

If the carrier places you in a higher class than purchased, it may not charge any additional payment.

---

# 6 PASSENGERS WITH REDUCED MOBILITY OR SPECIAL NEEDS

Carriers must give boarding priority to passengers with reduced mobility, persons accompanying them, certified service dogs, and unaccompanied children.

In the event of denied boarding, cancellation, or delay of any length, passengers with reduced mobility and their companions, as well as unaccompanied children, are entitled to care (meals, accommodation, transport, and communications) as soon as possible.

---

# 7 FURTHER COMPENSATION AND ENFORCEMENT

## 7.1 FURTHER COMPENSATION

The rights under this Regulation are minimum rights and do not prevent a passenger from claiming further compensation through national courts or other procedures. Any amount paid under this Regulation may be deducted from such additional compensation. This does not apply to passengers who voluntarily gave up their reservation.

Carriers' obligations under this Regulation cannot be waived or limited by any clause in the contract of carriage. If a passenger has accepted lower compensation as a result of not being properly informed of their rights, they retain the right to seek the difference before competent courts or bodies.

## 7.2 HOW TO COMPLAIN

Each EU Member State has designated a national enforcement body responsible for ensuring compliance with this Regulation at airports on its territory. Passengers may complain to that body about any alleged infringement on flights departing from that territory, or on flights from outside the EU arriving there.

Contact details for national enforcement bodies are available at: http://ec.europa.eu/transport/themes/passengers/air/doc/2004_261_national_enforcement_bodies.pdf

Carriers must display a clearly visible notice at check-in informing passengers of their rights, and must provide a written notice of rights to any passenger affected by denied boarding, cancellation, or a delay of at least two hours.
